Little Paws

Little Paws provides after school pet care in the South Bay Los Angeles area. Run by a group of students, the earnings of the organization are donated to the Leukemia and Lymphoma Society. To create a cohesive website that encourages smooth communicate with our clients, I used Figma to design the website.
From research, ideation, and to UI design and implementation, I designed the Little Paws webpage to display pet care staff, services, rates, operation time and contact information. I also created an input form to collect schedule book info from users. I chose a card-style and grid-based layout to help users access the contents easily. The typography hierarchy highlights the important information. I chose the primary color to be a light grayish orange color. This warm, low-contrast color gently welcomes viewers into the website. I designed a little paw image in an ellipse and heart shape on Figma, then inserted it into text for a simple logo. I designed it holistically by creating branding, logos and a color scheme to attract new clients.

South Bay Rice Things

This is a UI design for a Japanese restaurant's mobile app. I worked at South Bay Rice Things last summer and the restaurant mainly uses delivery services such as Grubhub and DoorDash for customers. However, I noticed that the restaurant lacked efficiency in their system of using tablets to alert the employees of online orders. Thus, I became inspired to create an app to help them with this issue.
I used Figma to create the UI design and Photoshop to edit food images. I picked out the typography and color palette. Black color is used as the primary color in contrast to the red logos. The background of buttons, active icons and part of the food images are red. The primary text color is white.
The app allows users to create accounts and keep order history. It helps customers to save time and speed the order process. After I put it together, I created several mockups to demonstrate the final design of the app. I plan to continue to improve this design in the future.
